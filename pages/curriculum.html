{% endif %} [Intro of curriculum] Remember that the workshops below provide more than open-access workshops. DHRI is a holistic approach to digital skill development that seeks to address a difficult and often elusive challenge in digital humanities training: combining critical, theoretically-engaged pedagogy with hands-on skill workshops, enabling emerging digital practitioners to become confident and flexible autodidacts through face-to-face workshops, centering contextual and localized knowledge production over the exoticism of “centers of expertise,” and doing so while supporting a robust human network of emerging DH leaders by connecting them with one another and external resources. Thus, we combine inclusive and critical pedagogy with a flexible set of workshops that foreground humanities skepticism and inquiry in form and content. We urge you to consider how your local context, pedagogical expectations, and professional responsibilities can and should impact the workshops below in your local contexts. Remember to not divide the skills learning below from humanistic inquiry. We all come to the learning experience as equals in our teaching and pedagogical questions, and these, in turn, should motivate the workshops. The approach is centered on celebrating the unique domains of knowledge that each participant and each institutional setting offers. Through explicit attention to inclusive language, instruction, and discussion, we encourage you to implement the digital skills workshops with a purposeful pedagogical approach. [Curriculum]
